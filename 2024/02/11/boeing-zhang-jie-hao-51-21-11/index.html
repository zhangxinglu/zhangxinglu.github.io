A. This procedure has these tasks:

(1)

(14) Chromium - Paint Stripping

(15) Copper-Plating - Paint Stripping

(16) Composite Parts - Paint Stripping

B. Paint strippers or solvents are used to remove small quantities of paint on metal surfaces. Do not

use paint stripper on plastic laminations, fiberglass, aerodynamic smoother, edges of metal bonds,

or rubber parts. Use only approved solvents for these materials.

CAUTION

DO NOT USE PAINT STRIPPER ON COMPOSITE MATERIALS. PAINT

STRIPPERS CAN CAUSE DAMAGE TO THE STRUCTURE OF THE PART.

(1) Use dry abrasive methods to remove the paint from the composite surfaces.

(a) For composites, the recommended abrasive is No. 240 Scotch-Brite. Use of alternative

blasting procedures for finish removal is structurally satisfactory. The procedure must

agree with the requirements given in SAE MA4872, “Paint Stripping of Commercial

Aircraft - Evaluation of Materials and Processes”. Refer to SOPM 20-30-02, or BAC5725,

“Stripping Organic Materials” for more information on paint stripping with blast media.

stripping. Paint strippers can be more effective if the aircraft surface is cleaned prior to paint

3.

Paint Stripping

A. General

(1)

This task has the following steps:

(a) Brush-on or paint-on paint stripping

(b) A removal of the epoxy adhesives and adhesive primers

(c) A removal of the rain erosion coating from fiberglass

(d) Selection of paint strippers.

CAUTION

DO NOT LET PAINT STRIPPERS TOUCH FIBREGLASS, ACRYLIC WINDOWS,

SEALANT FILLETS, TEFLON LINED SELF-LUBRICATED BEARINGS,

ELECTRICAL TERMINAL PLUGS, NYLON COATED WIRES AND NYLON

BUSHINGS. THE CHEMICAL COMPOUNDS IN THE PAINT STRIPPERS WILL

CAUSE DAMAGE TO THE COMPONENTS.

(2) Before you clean, you must protect the surfaces and parts which are easily damaged by paint

strippers.

(a) Make sure that the paint strippers do not fall on the joints.

(b) Do not use paint strippers in the fuel tanks.

(3) Before you remove the paint with the stripping procedure, make sure that the surface is clean

and dry.

(a) Make sure that the ambient temperature is between 50°F (10°C) and 100°F (38°C).

\1) Do not use the stripping procedure in hot or wet weather.

(b) Make sure that the paint stripping equipment are fully clean of chemical residue.

NOTE: Refer to equipment manufacturer’s instructions to clean paint stripping

equipment.

CAUTION

DO NOT USE PAINT STRIPPER ON COMPOSITE MATERIALS. PAINT

STRIPPERS CAN CAUSE DAMAGE TO THE STRUCTURE OF THE PART.

(4) Brush-on or spray-on stripping methods with paint strippers or solvents can be used on metal

surfaces.

(a) You can use a pressure sprayer gun, STD-447, or a brush to apply the paint strippers.

\1) The brush-on procedure is frequently used for line maintenance.

CAUTION

DO NOT USE THE SAME PUMPS AND HOSES WHEN MIXING DRUMS

OF DIFFERENT PAINT STRIPPERS THROUGH RECIRCULATION.

MIXING AND CROSS-CONTAMINATION CAN BE HIGHLY CORROSIVE

TO METAL SURFACES. THIS CAN CAUSE SKIN PITTING DAMAGE.

(b) Make sure that the components in the paint stripper are fully mixed.

\1) Do not use the same pumps and hoses when drums of different paint strippers are

mixed through recirculation.

a) Use dedicated pump and hose for different paint stripper type when drums of

paint striper are mixed through recirculation.
