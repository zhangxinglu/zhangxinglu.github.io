CONDUCTIVE COATING FOR EXTERNAL SURFACES - CLEANING/PAINTING

1. General

A. This procedure has this task:

(1)

Apply Conductive Coating to Specified External Surfaces.

B. When you apply conductive coatings to non-conductive, external airplane surfaces, it is to:

(1) Help discharge static electrical charges to the primary airplane structure, and

(2) Help the positive grounding of the static electrical charges to the airplane structure.

C. You can only use BMS10-21, Type II or IV for the Radome or HF antenna fiberglass panel. If you

use BMS10-21, Type III, the weather radar system or HF communication system will not operate

correctly.

SUBTASK 51-21-71-100-002

WARNING

DO NOT GET THIS MATERIAL IN YOUR MOUTH, EYES, OR ON YOUR SKIN.

CLEAN BARE SKIN FULLY AFTER YOU USE THIS MATERIAL. THIS

(1) Do this task: (Airplane Surface Preparation for Application of Finish, TASK 51-21-00-100-801).

SUBTASK 51-21-71-370-005

(2) Prepare the BMS 10–21 Type II conductive coating, C00841, with the steps as follows:

(a) Refer to the manufacturer’s instructions to mix the conductive coating.

(b) Fully shake each conductive coating component before you mix it.

NOTE: The conductive coating is supplied in two component kits. The kits have a base

component and a catalyst.

SUBTASK 51-21-71-370-006

(3) Apply the BMS 10–21 Type II conductive coating, C00841, compound with a paint spray gun

with interchangeable nozzles, STD-1133, or paint brush, STD-1080.

(a) Be sure that the temperature for the application of the coating, C00841, must be 40°F

(4°C) to 100°F (38°C). The preferred temperature is 50°F (10°C) to 90°F (32°C).

(b) The dry film thickness after it has been cured must be 0.0008 ±0.0002 in.

(0.0203 ±0.0051 mm).

SUBTASK 51-21-71-370-007

(4) Cure the BMS 10–21 Type II conductive coating, C00841, with the steps that follow:

(a) Allow the conductive coating that is in fastener holes cure for at least 20 minutes at 70°F

(21°C) to 90°F (32°C) before you install the fasteners.

(b) Heat-cure the anti-static coating before taking resistivity measurements or overcoating.

\1) The flash-off time necessary before the higher temperature cure is 15 minutes

minimum at 75 ±15°F (24 ±8°C).

SUBTASK 51-21-71-700-005

(a)

Make sure the conductive coating shows in the fastener location.

(b) Clean the area with solvent, B00083.

(c) Install one temporary fastener at each fastener location to be measured. (Figure 702).

NOTE: This procedure can be used for measuring electrical resistivity of panels with

covered (painted) conductive coatings when the ground fastener locations show.

(d)

Connect the digital/analog multimeter, COM-1793, probes at the two fastener locations as

shown in (Figure 702).

(e) Measure the resistivity with the digital/analog multimeter, COM-1793.

\1) The minimum permitted resistivity is no less than 1 Megohm/square.

\2) The maximum permitted resistivity is 100 Megohms/square.

(f) Remove the temporary fasteners from the surface.

(7)

Use one of the procedures follow to measure the resistivity of the conductive coating (Square

Test Method): (Figure 703)

NOTE: The term ohms/square is used to give the resistance in ohms of current passing from

one side of a square region to the opposite side, regardless of the size of the square.

NOTE: Do not use this procedure to measure the resistivity of conductive finishes when the

(a)

If the surface resistivity measurement probe, SPL-7885, is used on the conductive

coating surface, do the steps that follow:

\1) Put the digital/analog multimeter, COM-1793, with the surface resistivity

measurement probe, SPL-7885, in direct contact with the conductive coating

surface.

\2) Measure the resistivity the digital/analog multimeter, COM-1793.

a) The measured value multiplied by 10 gives the value of resistivity in

ohms/square.

NOTE: The term ohms/square is used because it gives the resistance in

ohms of current passing from one side of a square region to the

opposite side, regardless of the size of the square.

NOTE: The minimum resistivity is no less than 1 Megohm/square or a

maximum resistivity of 100 Megohms/square.

(b)

If the 3M 1181 copper foil tape, G50630, is used on the conductive coating surface, do

the steps that follow:

\1) Put two pieces of 3M 1181 copper foil tape, G50630, (of equal length (L)) parallel to

each other on the conductive coating surface.

\2) The two strips of 3M 1181 copper foil tape, G50630, must be put parallel to each

other a distance of one tenth the length of the two strips (0.1L ±5 percent).

\3) Push the two strips of 3M 1181 copper foil tape, G50630, against the conductive

coating.

\4) Measure the resistivity with the digital/analog multimeter, COM-1793.

a) The measured value multiplied by 10 gives the value of resistivity in

ohms/square.

NOTE: The term ohms/square is used because it gives the resistance in

ohms of current passing from one side of a square region to the

opposite side, regardless of the size of the square.

NOTE: The minimum resistivity is no less than 1 Megohm/square or a

maximum resistivity of 100 Megohms/square.

\5)

The measured value multiplied by 10 gives the value of resistivity in ohms/square.

\6) Remove the 3M 1181 copper foil tape, G50630, from the surface.

(8) Do the steps that follow if the resistivity of the conductive coating is less than 1

NOTE: If the abrasive cloth, B00102, is not available, use a abrasive mat, G00251, or

240 grit or finer abrasive paper, G50077, as an alternate.

SUBTASK 51-21-71-100-001

WARNING

DO NOT GET PAINT MATERIALS AND SOLVENTS IN YOUR MOUTH, IN

YOUR EYES, ON YOUR SKIN, OR ON YOUR CLOTHES. DO NOT BREATHE

THE FUMES FROM THESE MATERIALS. MAKE SURE THE AIR FLOWS

FREELY THROUGH THE WORK AREA. USE THE NECESSARY

RESPIRATORY PROTECTION. KEEP THESE MATERIALS AWAY FROM

SPARKS, FLAME, AND TEMPERATURES ABOVE THE FLASH POINT. THESE

MATERIALS ARE POISONOUS AND FLAMMABLE AND CAN CAUSE INJURY

TO PERSONS OR DAMAGE TO EQUIPMENT.

(1) Do this task: Airplane Surface Preparation for Application of Finish, TASK 51-21-00-100-801.

SUBTASK 51-21-71-370-001

CAUTION

DO NOT USE BMS10-21, TYPE III ON THE RADOME OR THE FIBERGLASS

PANEL FOR THE HF ANTENNA. YOU CAN ONLY USE BMS10-21, TYPE II OR

IV ON THESE. IF YOU USE BMS10-21, TYPE III, THE WEATHER RADAR

SYSTEM, AND THE HF COMMUNICATION SYSTEM WILL NOT OPERATE

CORRECTLY.

(2) Prepare the BMS 10-21 Type III conductive coating, C00767, with the steps as follows:

(a) Refer to the manufacturer’s instructions to mix the conductive coating.

(b) Fully shake each conductive coating component before you mix it.

NOTE: The conductive coating is supplied in two component kits. The kits have a base

component and a catalyst.

(c)

Put a label on the container with, the data that follows, immediately after it is mixed:

\1) The date and time it was mixed,

\2) The application time expiration time is as follows:

a) 4 hours if the temperature is less than 81°F (27°C)

b) 2 hours if the temperature is 81°F (27°C) to 100°F (38°C).

(d) Do not use the conductive coating for a minimum of 30 minutes after it is mixed.

SUBTASK 51-21-71-370-002

(3) Apply the BMS 10-21 Type III conductive coating, C00767, compound with a paint spray gun

with interchangeable nozzles, STD-1133, or paint brush, STD-1080.

(a) Be sure that the temperature for the application of the coating, C00767, must be 40°F

(4°C) to 100°F (38°C). The preferred temperature is 50°F (10°C) to 90°F (32°C).

(b) The dry film thickness after it has been cured must be 0.0006 ±0.0002 in.

(0.0152 ±0.0051 mm).

SUBTASK 51-21-71-370-003

(4) Cure the BMS 10-21 Type III conductive coating, C00767, with one of the steps that follow:

(a) Let the conductive coating that is in fastener holes cure for at least 20 minutes at 70°F

(21°C) to 90°F (32°C) before you install the fasteners.

(b) Air dry the surface of the conductive coating for 2 hours at 60°F (16°C).

(c) To cure the conductive coating quickly, apply a minimum temperature of 140°F (60°C)

before you do the resistivity measurements (Figure 701).

NOTE: The increased drying time as shown in Figure 701 is not a full cure.

D. Procedure

SUBTASK 51-21-71-100-003

WARNING

DO NOT GET THIS MATERIAL IN YOUR MOUTH, EYES, OR ON YOUR SKIN.

CLEAN BARE SKIN FULLY AFTER YOU USE THIS MATERIAL. THIS

MATERIAL CAN CAUSE INJURIES TO PERSONNEL OR DAMAGE TO

EQUIPMENT.

(1) Do this task: (Airplane Surface Preparation for Application of Finish, TASK 51-21-00-100-801).

SUBTASK 51-21-71-370-010

(2) Prepare the BMS 10–21 Type IV conductive coating, C50219, with the steps as follows:

(a) Refer to the manufacturer’s instructions to mix the conductive coating.

(b) Fully shake each conductive coating component before you mix it.

NOTE: The conductive coating is supplied in two component kits. The kits have a base

component and a catalyst.

SUBTASK 51-21-71-370-011

(3) Apply the BMS 10–21 Type IV conductive coating, C50219, compound with a paint spray gun

with interchangeable nozzles, STD-1133, or paint brush, STD-1080.

(a) Be sure that the temperature for the application of the coating, C50219, must be 40°F

(4°C) to 100°F (38°C). The preferred temperature is 50°F (10°C) to 90°F (32°C).

(b) The dry film thickness after it has been cured must be 0.0008 ±0.0002 in.

(0.0203 ±0.0051 mm).

SUBTASK 51-21-71-370-012

(4) Cure the BMS 10–21 Type IV conductive coating, C50219, with the steps that follow:

(a) Allow the conductive coating that is in fastener holes cure for at least 20 minutes at 70°F

(21°C) to 90°F (32°C) before you install the fasteners.

(b) Heat-cure the anti-static coating before taking resistivity measurements or overcoating.

\1) The flash-off time necessary before the higher temperature cure is 15 minutes

minimum at 75 ±15°F (24 ±8°C).

\2) Apply heat to cure the conductive coating before you do the resistivity

measurements as shown in (Figure 701).

NOTE: The increased drying time as shown in Figure 701 is not a full cure.

a) Do not cure above 160°F (71°C).

b)

Do not cure at temperatures of 120°F (49°C) or above for longer than 2 hours.

\3) The cool-off time necessary before subsequent processing the cure is 15 minutes

minimum at 75 ±15°F (24 ±8°C).

(5)

Do the steps that follow to measure the resistivity of the conductive coating

(Fastener-to-Conductive Surface Test Method):

NOTE: The resistivity of the conductive coating after it is cured must be no less than 1

Megohm/square or more than 100 Megohms/square.

NOTE: Let the conductive coat cure for the specified time as shown in (Figure 701) before you

measure the conductivity.

(a)

If the bonding surface and the head of the fastener are covered with a finish, (Figure 702)

do the steps as follows:

\1) Remove a sufficient quantity of the outer coating or paint to let the probes touch the

conductive finish.

NOTE: If it is necessary, you can push the probes through the outer coating.

\2) Put the digital/analog multimeter, COM-1793, with the film resistance measure

probe, SPL-10361, in direct contact with the bonding fastener and the conductive

finish.

a) Put the film resistance measure probe, SPL-10361, as shown in Figure 702.

\3) Make a record of the value of the resistivity shown on the multimeter.

a) The minimum permitted resistivity is no less than 1 Megohm/square.

b) The maximum permitted resistivity is 100 Megohms/square.

SUBTASK 51-21-71-700-008

(6) Do the steps that follow to measure the resistivity of the conductive coating on uninstalled

panels (Fastener-to-Fastener Test Method):

NOTE: The resistivity of the conductive coating after it is cured must be no less than 1

Megohm/square or more than 100 Megohms/square.

(a)

Make sure the conductive coating shows in the fastener location.

(b) Clean the area with solvent, B00083.

(c) Install one temporary fastener at each fastener location to be measured. (Figure 702).

NOTE: This procedure can be used for measuring electrical resistivity of panels with

covered (painted) conductive coatings when the ground fastener locations show.

(d)

Connect the digital/analog multimeter, COM-1793, probes at the two fastener locations as

shown in (Figure 702).

(e) Measure the resistivity with the digital/analog multimeter, COM-1793.

\1) The minimum permitted resistivity is no less than 1 Megohm/square.

\2) The maximum permitted resistivity is 100 Megohms/square.

(f) Remove the temporary fasteners from the surface.

SUBTASK 51-21-71-700-009

(7) Use one of the procedures that follow to measure the resistivity of the conductive coating

(Square Test Method): (Figure 703)

NOTE: The term ohms/square is used to give the resistance in ohms of current passing from

one side of a square region to the opposite side, regardless of the size of the square.

NOTE: Do not use this procedure to measure the resistivity of conductive finishes when the

surface is covered (painted).

Valid until 7days after A/C N307FL is deliveryed(a) If the surface resistivity measurement probe, SPL-7885, is used on the conductive

coating surface, do the steps that follow:

\1) Put the digital/analog multimeter, COM-1793, with the surface resistivity

measurement probe, SPL-7885 in direct contact with the conductive coating

surface.

\2) Measure the resistivity the digital/analog multimeter, COM-1793.

a) The measured value multiplied by 10 gives the value of resistivity in

ohms/square.

NOTE: The term ohms/square is used because it gives the resistance in

ohms of current passing from one side of a square region to the

opposite side, regardless of the size of the square.

NOTE: The minimum resistivity is no less than 1 Megohm/square or a

maximum resistivity of 100 Megohms/square.

(b)

If the 3M 1181 copper foil tape, G50630, is used on the conductive coating surface, do

the steps that follow:

\1) Put two pieces of 3M 1181 copper foil tape, G50630, (of equal length (L)) parallel to

each other on the conductive coating surface.

\2) The two strips of 3M 1181 copper foil tape, G50630, must be put parallel to each

other a distance of one tenth the length of the two strips (0.1L ±5 percent).

\3) Push the two strips of 3M 1181 copper foil tape, G50630, against the conductive

coating.

\4) Measure the resistivity with the digital/analog multimeter, COM-1793.

a) The measured value multiplied by 10 gives the value of resistivity in

ohms/square.

NOTE: The term ohms/square is used because it gives the resistance in

ohms of current passing from one side of a square region to the

opposite side, regardless of the size of the square.

NOTE: The minimum resistivity is no less than 1 Megohm/square or a

maximum resistivity of 100 Megohms/square.

\5)

The measured value multiplied by 10 gives the value of resistivity in ohms/square.

\6) Remove the 3M 1181 copper foil tape, G50630, from the surface.

SUBTASK 51-21-71-370-013

(8) Do the steps that follow if the resistivity of the conductive coating is less than 1

Megohm/Square:

(a) Use 240 grit or finer abrasive cloth, B00102, paper to remove the conductive coating.

NOTE: If the abrasive cloth, B00102, is not available, use a abrasive mat, G00251, or

240 grit or finer abrasive paper, G50077, as an alternate.

(b)

Remove the remaining particles with a clean cotton wiper, G00034, that is moist with

solvent, B00083.

(c) Dry the surface with a clean cotton wiper, G00034, before the solvent is dry.

(d) Do the steps to apply a new layer of conductive coating.

(e) Do the steps to cure the conductive coating.

(f) Do the steps to measure the resistivity of the new layer of conductive coating.
