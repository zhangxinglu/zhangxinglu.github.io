51-76-12 - Description - REPAIR OF FUEL TANK SEALING

51-76-12 - 描述-油箱密封的修复

WARNING: BE CAREFUL WHEN YOU USE CONSUMABLE MATERIALS. OBEY THE MATERIAL MANUFACTURER INSTRUCTIONS AND YOUR LOCAL REGULATIONS.

WARNING: YOU MUST OBEY ALL THE APPLICABLE FUEL SAFETY PROCEDURES GIVEN IN AMM CHAPTER 28-00-00-03 CONF 00. FOR WORK IN THE FUEL TANKS, REFER TO THE FUEL SAFETY PROCEDURES GIVEN IN AMM CHAPTER 28-10-00-03 CONF 00.

WARNING: BEFORE YOU DO WORK IN OR NEAR A FUEL TANK, MAKE SURE THAT YOU OBEY ALL THE SAFETY PROCEDURES. THIS WILL PREVENT INJURY TO PERSONS AND/OR DAMAGE TO THE AIRCRAFT.

WARNING: BEFORE YOU DO WORK ON THE FUEL SYSTEM, MAKE SURE THAT YOU OBEY THE FUEL SAFETY PROCEDURES. THIS WILL PREVENT INJURY TO PERSONS AND/OR DAMAGE TO THE AIRCRAFT.

CAUTION: DO NOT USE METAL TOOLS TO REMOVE THE UNWANTED MATERIAL. METAL TOOLS CAN CAUSE DAMAGE TO THE SURFACE OF THE COMPONENT.

CAUTION: MAKE SURE THAT YOU REMOVE ALL MASKING MATERIAL FROM THE REPAIR AREA WHEN THE WORK IS COMPLETED. IT IS VERY IMPORTANT TO MAKE SURE THAT THERE IS NO MASKING MATERIAL ON THE PITOT PROBE HEADS AND STATIC PORTS.

1.Task Supporting Data

A.General

(1) One of the properties of aircraft fuel is that it can flow easily through very small openings. Thus the aircraft structure in which the fuel is kept is sealed to prevent leakage.

(2) If the sealant in these areas is damaged or becomes defective, a fuel leakage may occur, this can be dangerous. When a leak occurs, you must only use the correct sealants and procedures for the necessary repair.

(3) This task gives you the information necessary to do a permanent repair to stop a leakage of fuel. The information for a temporary repair, for which it is not necessary to go into the tank is given in the AMM Chapter 51-76-12.

(4) Fuel leak reporting

When reporting a fuel leak to AIRBUS, please use the fuel leak reporting form, given in Figure 010.

Procedure

A. Repair of Fuel Tank Sealing

NOTE: For specific fuel leak repairs, refer to Paragraph 2..A..(5).

(1) The Source of the Fuel Leakage

(a) You are recommended to use the procedures given in the AMM Chapter 28-11-00-05 CONF 00 - Tanks - Adjustment/Test to find the source.

(b) When you have found the area which is the source of the fuel leakage, you must examine it carefully. You must find if the cause of the leakage is:

-failed or damaged sealant

-a structural problem, such as a crack, or a fastener which has failed.

If a structural problem is the cause of the leakage, you must repair it before you repair the sealant.

(2) Removal of Damaged Sealant

CAUTION: DO NOT USE A METAL SCRAPER TO REMOVE THE UNWANTED MATERIAL. A METAL SCRAPER CAN CAUSE DAMAGE TO THE SURFACE OF THE COMPONENT.

(a) When you have found the area which is the source of the fuel leakage (Refer to Paragraph 2..A..(1)

), you must carefully remove all the damaged sealant. When you remove the damaged sealant, take care not to cause damage to the structure. Use a non−metallic scraper made from hard wood, plastic or laminated fibre block (Refer to Sheet

1). As an alternative removal procedure it is recommended to use a pneumatic tool with a vibrating cutting blade made of teak peek (Refer to Sheet 2).

For this specific tool refer to manufacturer instructions:

NOTE: Remove sealant particles and inspect visually the surface protection of the structure.

(b) If the surface protection on the structure is damaged, you must repair it before you repair the seal (Refer to Chapter 51-24-00 for the applicable procedure).

NOTE: If it is necessary to renew the surface protection, the new protection must be allowed to cure before applying sealant. After protection curing it is necessary to abrade the protection lightly to improve sealant adhesion.

(3) Cleaning the Repair Area

(a) It is very important that the area which you will seal is clean before you apply the sealant. You must wear clean gloves on your hand if it is necessary to touch a surface which you have cleaned. Make sure that the tools you use to apply the sealant are clean before you start.

(b) To clean an area you must put sufficient non aqueous cleaner (Material No. 08BBF1 or product code 864400) on a lint-free cloth to make the cloth moist. Only use the minimum quantity necessary or you can cause damage to the paint. Also clean an area of at least 50 mm (1.97 in) around the repair area.

(c) After cleaning wait 15−20 minutes to allow the surface of the area to return to ambient temperature. After that, wipe the surface with a clean dry cloth to remove any condensation.

(4) The Use of Sealants

(a) General

CAUTION: BE CAREFUL WHEN THE FUEL TANK IS OPEN. DO NOT LET SEALANT OR OTHER UNWANTED MATERIAL GET INTO THE FUEL AND/OR AIR PASSAGES IN THE FUEL TANK.

1 To make or to repair a seal with sealant, it is very important that you use the correct procedures. If you do not, another leakage could occur. In the steps which follow you are given the general procedure to use when you apply sealants. You must use this procedure together with the applicable repair procedure.

a You must lightly abrade all surfaces. This will help the sealant to make a good bond.

NOTE: The above mentioned step is not necessary, if the surfaces have a new layer of paint or primer (Refer to Paragraph

2..A..(2).(b) ).

b The use of adhesion promoter is necessary, if the CML application code providing the sealant calls also an adhesion promoter. This will promote good sealant adhesion and therefore reduce the risk of a further leakage occurring.

c You must put a cover over mixed sealant when it is not in use.

d

You must discard sealant when its “application life” expires and it has started to cure. Do not try to make it thin.

e If you have kept mixed sealant in a refrigerator, remove it from the refrigerator and let it get to “room temperature” before you use it. Do not put unwanted sealant back into the refrigerator.

(b) Local and General Internal Repair (Refer to Figure 003).

1 The repair that follows is suitable for a quick permanent repair when a leak occurs from a seam, channel or a fastener.

a Observe all relevant safety precautions and procedures before entering the tank (Refer to AMM Chapter 28-10-00).

b Do a leak path analysis to determine the cause of the leak.

c Remove sealant from the affected area using a non-metallic scraper.

d Clean the exposed surfaces with a lint-free cloth moistened with non aqueous cleaner (Material No. 08BBF1 or product code 864400).

e Inspect the area for cracks and other defects.

NOTE: Any defects found must be repaired at this stage.

f With a sealant gun or spatula, apply a bead of polysulfide sealant (Material No. 06ABF1) to the seam, channel or fastener. Press the bead of sealant firmly into the seam or channel and around any fastener.

Make sure that a smooth fillet, free from air bubbles, is formed. Overlap existing sealant.

g Make sure that the inside of the tank is clear of all unwanted materials and that all tools and equipment have been removed.

h Close the tank, refuel fully and check for leaks.

NOTE: If no barrier coating has been used, allow time to partially cure the sealant before refuelling.

If the original bolt was an interference fit you must replace it with an applicable oversize bolt, (Refer to Chapter 51-40-00 for the details on fastener installation and removal, applicable oversize bolts and torque values). (5) Specific Fuel Leak Repairs

(a) For specific fuel leak repairs contact AIRBUS.

(6) Quick Curing Sealant Device

NOTE: Obey the related repair procedures and safety precautions, given in this section, carefully.

(a) The new sealant must have the correct state of cure before the refuelling starts. If possible, the sealant should cure to a hardness of 35 Shore A. To determine what this cure time is, the operator takes a test sample and uses a hardness gage to measure the period of time necessary to get a hardness of 35 shore A.

To date and for practical purposes, AIRBUS recommends that the time before refuelling should be the shortest of:

-A minimum of two times the tack free time (the tack free time is the time necessary for the sealant to become “touch dry “)

-the cure time.

The tack free time and the cure time decrease as the temperature increases. AIRBUS thus investigated different devices to increase and control the temperature in the repair area (50° C (122° F) maximum). Devices such as hot air blowers, infra-red lamps and thermoreactor technology, which uses catalytic combustion can be used.

Refer to Chapter 51-76-00 for description and information on the thermoreactor.
