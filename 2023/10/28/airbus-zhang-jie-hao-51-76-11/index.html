51-76-11 - Description - REPAIR OF SEALING EXCLUDING FUEL TANK

51-76-11 -描述-密封件的维修，不包括燃料箱

WARNING: BE CAREFUL WHEN YOU USE CONSUMABLE MATERIALS. OBEY THE MATERIAL MANUFACTURER INSTRUCTIONS AND YOUR LOCAL REGULATIONS.

CAUTION: DO NOT USE METAL TOOLS TO REMOVE THE UNWANTED MATERIAL. METAL TOOLS CAN CAUSE DAMAGE TO THE SURFACE OF THE COMPONENT.

1.General

A.This task tells you how to repair a seal when it becomes necessary. Figure 001shows you some examples of the use of sealants. The task is divided into these parts:

-General,

-Safety Precautions,

-Repair Procedures.

B.The Repair Procedures are divided into:

-General,

-Fastener Sealing,

-Anchor Nut Sealing,

-Sealing Gaps and Cavities,

-Interfaying Surface Seal,

-How to Make and Repair a Bead Seal.

2.Safety Precautions

A.Sealants and their related materials are usually made from chemicals. These chemicals can release dangerous gases that can cause damage to the eyes, the lungs and the skin. When you use these materials, obey these warnings:

WARNING: OBEY THE MANUFACTURER’S INSTRUCTIONS WHEN YOU USE CLEANING AGENTS, ADHESIVES, SEALANTS AND PAINTS. THESE MATERIALS ARE DANGEROUS.

WARNING: OBEY THE MANUFACTURER INSTRUCTIONS WHEN YOU USE THE CLEANING AGENTS (MATERIAL NO. 08BBF1). THESE CLEANING AGENTS ARE DANGEROUS.

WARNING: BE CAREFUL WHEN YOU APPLY CHROMIC ACID ANODIZING (CAA) AND CHEMICAL CONVERSION COATING. THE CHEMICALS USED ARE POISONOUS AND CAN CAUSE INJURY. SEE THE MATERIAL SAFETY DATA SHEETS.

CAUTION: ENSURE THAT ALL MASKING MATERIALS ARE REMOVED FROM THE REPAIR AREA ON COMPLETION OF WORK, PAY SPECIAL ATTENTION TO PITOT HEADS AND STATIC PORTS.

B. When you do repair work with sealants in a closed area, you must also take these precautions:

-Use air circulation equipment, or a clean-air mask,

-Use a safety man to monitor you while you are in the closed area. The safety man must, if possible, be outside the closed area, but he must be able to see and talk to you,

-Do not smoke, or use open lamps in the closed or adjacent areas,

- You must only use approved flame-proof lamps fitted with bulbs of 40 watts or less,

-Before you do a repair in an area near a moving part or an electrical circuit, safety the system or electrical circuits.

Figure 001 / 51-76-11 (sheet 1) Examples of the Use of Sealants

C.Before you do any work with sealants or their related materials, you must ground this equipment:

-the structure,

-the extrusion gun,

-the air circulation line,

-metal parts of the clean-air mask,

-flame proof lamps.

You must measure the ground points before you use them. You do this to make sure they have the correct conductivity.

4.Repair Procedures

A.General

(1) Put masking tape around the damaged sealant. You do this to prevent damage to the undamaged sealant and the adjacent structure.

(2) Use nonmetallic scrapers and a stiff brush to remove the damaged sealant.

(3) Chamfer the ends of the remaining sealant to give a minimum taper of 13 mm (0.512 in). Refer to Figure 002. Make the faces of the tapers rough.

NOTE: The tapers give a good overlap for the new sealant to bond onto the remaining sealant.

(4) Clean the repair area with the non aqueous cleaner (Material No. 08BBF1 or 864400). Use as little non aqueous cleaner as possible to clean the area. Dry the area immediately with a clean dry cloth.

CAUTION: IF YOU MUST DO ANY REPAIR WORK IN THE STEP BELOW, A PERIOD OF 8 HOURS IS NECESSARY BEFORE YOU CAN DO THE SEALANT REPAIR.

(5) Do an inspection of the repair area to see if the protective finish is damaged. Repair it, if it is damaged. Refer to Chapter 51-23-00 for the repair information.

(6) Prepare the correct sealant. Put a layer of the sealant on the repair area. Use a spatula or extrusion gun to do this. Make sure that there are no air bubbles under the sealant. Make sure that the sealant has made a good bond at the overlaps.

(7) Apply the correct protective layer to the sealant, if necessary (Refer to Chapter 51-23-12).

Figure 002 / 51-76-11 (sheet 1) Example of Steps in a Bead Repair

B.Fastener Sealing (Refer to Figure 003)

(1) This procedure is applicable to solid rivets.

(a) Clean the area you want to seal with the non aqueous cleaner (Material No. 08BBF1 or 864400).

(b) Apply a layer of the sealant to the inside face of the hole. Make a small bead of the sealant around the edge of the hole. If the rivet is a countersink type, apply a layer of the sealant to the face of the dimple or countersink in the structure.

NOTE: When the upset head of a rivet is made, the shank of the rivet will expand. This effect will make a seal in the structure.

(2) This procedure is applicable to clearance and/or transition fit fasteners, for example, HI-LOK and swaged pins.

CAUTION: DO NOT CLEAN THESE TYPES OF FASTENERS, OR THEIR COLLARS. THEY HAVE A SPECIAL LUBRICANT ON THEM WHICH IS IMPORTANT FOR THEIR CORRECT ASSEMBLY.

(a)Clean the area you want to seal with the non aqueous cleaner (Material No. 08BBF1 or 864400).

(b) Apply a layer of the sealant to the plain part of the pin shank and the underface of the head.

NOTE: Make sure that sealant does not get on the threads or serrations of the pin.

(c) Install the pin and its collar.

(d) Apply a layer of sealant to the broken end of the collar or pin, if required.

(e) Remove unwanted sealant from the repair area.

(f) Clean the repair area with the non aqueous cleaner (Material No. 08BBF1 or 864400).

(g) Apply the correct surface protection to the pin head, collar and threaded area. Refer to Chapter 51-75-12.

(3) This procedure is applicable to interference fit bolts.

(a) Clean the area you want to seal with the non aqueous cleaner (Material No. 08BBF1 or 864400).

(b) Apply a layer of sealant to the surface of the repair materials that the set head of the bolt will contact.

NOTE: Make sure that sealant does not get on the threads or serrations of the pin.

(c) Install the pin and its collar.

Figure 003 / 51-76-11 (sheet 1) Examples of Procedures Used to Prevent Leaks

(d) Apply a layer of sealant to the broken end of the collar or pin, if necessary.

(e) Remove unwanted sealant from the repair area.

(f) Clean the repair area with the non aqueous cleaner (Material No. 08BBF1 or 864400).

(g) Apply the correct surface protection to the pin head, collar and threaded area. Refer to Chapter 51-75-12.

C. Anchor Nut Sealing

(1) After you have installed the anchor nut, clean the anchor nut and the adjacent area with the non aqueous cleaner (Material No. 08BBF1 or 864400).

(2) Apply a layer of sealant to the anchor nut and the adjacent structure. Refer to Figure 003.

NOTE: If the anchor nut is a two-part type, the sealant must include all the anchor nut. If the anchor nut is a cap-type, it is only necessary to include the base.

D. Sealing Gaps and Cavities

For seal the limits to gaps and cavities in external surfaces refer to Chapter 51-10-00.

E. Interfaying Surface Seal (Refer to Figure 004)

When you repair the structure, it is usually necessary to make a seal between the parts (Interfaying Seal). The steps below give the general procedure if it is not given in the repair procedure.

NOTE: When possible, you must do all machining steps (for example, drilling and reaming) before you apply the sealant. If this is not possible, make sure that the repair pieces are clamped together tightly when you do the machining steps. This will prevent a bad seal caused by swarf in the joint. Only use sharp tools and a light pressure when you do the machining steps.

(1) Lightly make the mating faces rough.

NOTE: It is not necessary to do step (1) if the paint or primer on these faces is new.

NOTE: Alternatively you can use adhesive promoter (Refer to CML for product reference if required as per selected sealant and wait for the necessary time before sealant application).

(2) Clean the mating faces with the non aqueous cleaner (Material No. 08BBF1 or 864400).

(3)With a spatula, or extrusion gun, apply a layer of sealant onto the mating face of the smaller part to be joined. Use a grooved roller, or a serrated plastic tool to get an equal level of sealant on the required mating face. The thickness of the applied sealant must be approximately 0.5 mm (0.020 in).

NOTE: Take care not to make air bubbles in the sealant.

For thick parts (above 2.5 mm (0.1 in) thick) attached with fasteners with a pitch greater than 5 x d (in mating face), you must use only Class C polysulfide sealant (Material No. 06AAC1, Material No. 06ACC1, Material No. 06ABC1) applied with roller to a thickness of 0.15 mm (0.006 in) to 0.38 mm (0.015 in).

(4) Assemble the repair pieces into their correct position. Make sure that no air is included in the joint and that sealant is subsequently squeezed out (for information: remaining thickness is approximately ≤ 0.2 mm (0.008 in), refer to Figure 008).

NOTE: Make the layer of sealant applied in step (3) thicker if the repair pieces must touch when they are moved into their positions.

Figure 004 / 51-76-11 (sheet 1) Examples of an Interfay Seal

(5) Use clamps or screw pins to hold the repair pieces in their positions.

For thick parts (above 2.5 mm (0.1 in) thick) attached with fasteners with a pitch greater than 5 x d (in mating face) install clamps or screw pins in every hole available starting from center then outward, refer to Figure 009. Adjust the clamping force to ensure a minimum gap at fastener hole, refer to Figure 008.

(6) Make sure that a continuous bead of sealant has been pushed out around all the edges of the repair parts.

NOTE: This will show you if a good seal has been made.

(7) Install the correct fasteners or rivets. Install them wet (Refer to Paragraph

4..B. unless it is specified differently in the repair procedure.

NOTE: This step must be done before the work life of the sealant has expired (Refer to Chapter 51-76-00).

(8) Fasteners which are torqued (refer to Chapter 51-49-00) when they are installed (for example, TAPER-LOK fasteners), must be re-torqued. Do this 30 minutes after they were installed. If there are more than two pieces in the repair joint, do the retorquing every 30 minutes until the fasteners are correctly torqued.

(9) Make the unwanted sealant into a bead around the joint edges (Refer to Paragraph

  1. Do this before the sealant has cured.

NOTE: A repair procedure may specify that unwanted sealant must be removed from a repair. The repair procedure must be obeyed.

(10) Cover the repair area with a polythene sheet to keep the sealant clean until it has cured.

(11) Apply the correct surface protection to the repair area (Refer to Chapter 51-75-12). If necessary, apply a protective layer to the sealant.

F. How to Make and Repair a Bead Seal (Refer to Figure 004, Figure 005, Figure 006 and Figure 007)

You must make a bead of sealant along the edges of a repair (or parts of the structure), if it is specified. The steps below give you the general procedure to do this, and to help repair a damaged bead.

(1) Clean the repair area with the non aqueous cleaner (Material No. 08BBF1 or 864400).

(2) Do the repair or assembly.

(3) Push the sealant tightly into any corners. Make sure that all air- bubbles are pushed out. Make sure that a good adhesion is made between the sealant and the faces.

NOTE: You can use a spatula or extrusion gun to apply the sealant.

(4) Make the sealant into a bead of the correct dimensions (Refer to Figure 005).

NOTE: A large bead must be made in layers. Each layer must be put on before the other has cured.

(5) Cover the repair with a polythene sheet to keep the sealant clean until it has cured.

(6) Apply the correct surface protection to the repair area (Refer to Chapter 51-75-12). If it is necessary, apply a flexible protective layer to the sealant (Material No. 04QAB2).
